<p><p>The Silicon Carbide Continuous Fibers market is segmented based on production methods. Preceramic Polymer Pyrolysis (3P) involves transforming preceramic polymers into fibers through pyrolysis, offering flexibility in fiber architecture. Chemical Vapor Deposited (CVD) fibers are created by depositing silicon carbide from gaseous precursors, yielding high purity and uniformity. Chemical Vapor Reaction (CVR) involves a chemical reaction that forms fibers directly from vaporized precursors, often resulting in enhanced properties. Other methods may include novel techniques or variations on these established processes.</p></p>
