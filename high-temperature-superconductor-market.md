<p><p>The high-temperature superconductor (HTS) market is categorized into two main types: 1G HTS and 2G HTS. 1G HTS refers to bulk superconductors, primarily made of materials like YBCO in wire form, offering limited flexibility and scalability but robust performance in specific applications. In contrast, 2G HTS consists of coated conductors that provide enhanced flexibility, higher current-carrying capacity, and improved performance for diverse applications, including energy transmission and magnets, driving their growing adoption in various industries.</p></p>
