<p><p>The disc brake caliper market includes two primary types: plug type and rotating type. The plug type calipers use a stationary design with a single or multi-piston mechanism that pushes brake pads against the disc to create friction. In contrast, rotating type calipers incorporate a rotating mechanism that can enhance braking efficiency and reduce weight. Each type offers distinct advantages depending on vehicle requirements, performance characteristics, and design preferences, catering to diverse automotive applications and driving styles.</p></p>
